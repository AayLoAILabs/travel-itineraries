General Advice (Family/Expert/2025)

  • Acclimatization is Essential: First 36h: slow, relax. Avoid alcohol/smokes. Hydrate with water (soup, butter tea good).
    Option: Gentle old Leh walk with local guide if you feel good by sunset.
  • ILP/PAP: Needed for Nubra, Pangong, Tso Moriri, Hanle, Turtuk for all Indians. PAP for foreigners (agency required). Bring 6+ copies.
  • AMS: Know symptoms‚Äîheadache, nausea, restless sleep. Drop altitude if severe. Diamox only if prescribed.
    Local: Butter tea and dried apricots may help but always defer to physician/medical advice.
  • Cash: ATMs only in Leh. Carry extra cash for outer regions (small change for monasteries/crafts).
  • Network: BSNL/Airtel postpaid only in Leh. No data outside Leh. Download offline maps and share full itinerary with someone at home.
  • Packing: Thermals, fleece & windproof, boots, powerbank, med kit, sunscreen, moisturizer/lip balm, quick-dry towel, snacks, insulated bottle.
  • Responsible: Please minimize plastic, buy direct from locals (crafts, jams, woolens). Ask before taking photos.
  • Car/Driver: Book an expert Ladakhi driver with SUV/MUV. Only drive yourself if you are expert in Himalayan conditions.
  • Kargil/Drass Sector: These regions require separate planning. This guide focuses on classic Ladakh circuits.
Family Pro-Tip: Avoid altitude jumps and maximize nights in Leh for the most comfortable (and low-risk) trip for all ages. Local cuisine is mild and delicious‚Äîask your hotel to arrange a family kitchen meal or Ladakhi cooking demo.
Recommended Stays (all tried/family-friendly):
  • Leh: The Grand Dragon, The Indus Valley
  • Nubra: Stone Hedge, Lchang Nang Retreat
  • Pangong: The Pangong Inn, Hermitage Pangong, or premium camps (confirm water/amenities)
Driver/Route: All high passes (Khardung La, Chang La, Hanle) need a very confident, local driver. Morning crossings are safest. Self-drive as a challenge only for veterans!
