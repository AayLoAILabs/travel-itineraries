Important General Advice for Your Ladakh Trip

  1. Acclimatization: Leh is at approx 11,500 ft. Spend day one (ideally two) with low activity. Avoid alcohol and smoking. Hydrate constantly.
  2. ILP (Inner Line Permit): Required for Indians (Nubra, Pangong, Tso Moriri, Hanle, Turtuk, etc) ‚Äî can get online or in Leh. Foreigners PAP (via operator). Carry many photocopies.
  3. AMS: Know symptoms (headache, dizziness, nausea, fatigue). Descend and seek help if serious. Diamox can be considered (~consult your doctor).
  4. Cash: ATMs only in Leh, may run dry. Take enough for the whole circuit (including small notes).
  5. Connectivity: Patchy. BSNL/Airtel postpaid best in Leh, Jio at a few spots. Internet may be slow.
  6. Packing essentials:
    • Thermal, fleece, windproof jacket, woolen cap/gloves/socks.
    • Sunscreen, sunglasses, wide-brimmed hat.
    • Sturdy walking shoes. Pain relievers, stomach meds, band-aids, etc.
    • Reusable water bottle, power bank, lip balm, moisturizer.
  7. Responsible Tourism: Respect local culture. Avoid litter, minimize plastic, always ask before photos.
  8. Car & Driver: Hire a skilled local (SUV/MUV). Self-drive only if you have Himalayan experience.
  9. Kargil/Drass: Not in below itineraries. Separate trip/extension needed for Suru, Zojila, War Memorial.
